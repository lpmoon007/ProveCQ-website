GWC · the weak link

Capacity is the one GWC box you're guessing on.

EOS is a great operating system, and Prove is not a replacement for it. But there's one spot where even disciplined teams run on a hunch: the Capacity in GWC. It gets treated as a checkbox — and that's where good people get mis-placed.

The short answer

Gets it and Wants it reveal themselves over time. Capacity gets a yes/no checkbox, and it's often self-reported — and “I have capacity” frequently means “I want to please my boss,” not “I can actually carry this.” Prove fills that gap for EOS by measuring behavior over time, which is much harder to fake than a checkbox.

Why the Capacity box fails

It’s self-reported

People say yes to please you, to seem capable, or because they genuinely believe it — none of which means they can deliver.

It’s a single moment

A checkbox is a snapshot. Capacity is dynamic — it shifts with life stage, bandwidth, and unseen stress.

It absorbs the other letters

Leaders mark Capacity down when they really mean the person doesn’t Get it or Want it — muddying the whole People Analyzer.

How behavior reveals capacity

Voluntary action

Does the person take action without being told? Initiative is capacity showing itself.

Follow-through over time

Do they finish the hard parts, repeatedly? Sustained follow-through is capacity you can trust.

Hard to fake

One good week is easy to stage. Weeks of demonstrated commitment behavior are not. That’s the signal.

Important nuance: if someone doesn’t show the behavior, it doesn’t prove they lack capacity. But if they do — consistently, under pressure — that’s a strong, hard-to-fake indication the capacity is real. Prove turns the Capacity checkbox into evidence.
