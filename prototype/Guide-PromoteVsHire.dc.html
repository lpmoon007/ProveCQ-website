People decisions

Promote from within, or hire outside?

Both paths have a failure mode. Promote the wrong insider and you lose a great doer and gain a struggling manager. Hire the wrong outsider and you pay a premium for a culture misfit. Proof breaks the tie.

The short answer

Promote from within when an internal candidate has already proven the behavior the new role needs; hire outside when no one has and the gap is too costly to coach. The decision should hinge on demonstrated capacity, not tenure or convenience.

When to promote from within

An insider has proven the behavior

They already show the initiative, applied grit, and learnability the new role demands.

Culture & context matter most

The role needs deep institutional knowledge more than a new skill set.

When to hire outside

No proven internal candidate

Nobody has shown the behavior, and the cost of a failed promotion is too high to gamble.

You need a capability you lack

The gap is a genuine skill the team cannot coach into someone in time.
