Hiring

How to hire people who actually follow through.

The candidate who interviews best is often the one who performs worst. Interviews test how well someone talks about work — not whether they do it when no one is watching.

The short answer

Stop hiring on the interview alone. Score candidates on observed behavior — through work samples, trial tasks, and a structured behavioral read — so you hire the doer, not the charmer. What someone does under a small real demand predicts what they will do under a big one.

Why interviews mislead you

01

Charisma ≠ commitment

Confidence and polish are easy to fake in a 45-minute conversation.

02

Past titles ≠ future effort

A great resume tells you where someone has been, not how hard they will work for you.

03

One moment, not a pattern

Interviews are a single snapshot. Follow-through is a pattern you can only see over time.

What to measure instead

Initiative

Did they do more than the minimum in the application and trial task?

Applied Grit

Did they push through friction, or fade when it got tedious?

Learnability

Did they absorb feedback and improve inside the process?
