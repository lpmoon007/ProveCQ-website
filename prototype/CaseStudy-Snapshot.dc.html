The deliverable, in full

A CQ Snapshot, exactly as you'd receive it

This is a sample report for a team of HVAC technicians facing a promotion decision. Three people, three behaviors, one clear read on who's ready for more scope — and who needs structure first.

How to read it

CQ scores three behaviors under pressure, each 1–10 — Initiative (who acts without being told), Applied Grit (who stays with it when it's hard), and Learnability (who adapts and grows). The CQ Score is the average.

🟢 Green — ready for more scope 🟡 Yellow — growth potential with coaching 🔴 Red — needs structure before a stretch

Low CQ ≠ low performance

Some of your best people will score lower on CQ. It measures behavioral readiness under pressure — not talent, skill, or worth. A low score simply signals someone may need more structure, clarity, or support before stepping into a higher-scope or leadership role; many thrive in well-defined roles.

This report is a confidential behavioral snapshot for leadership development, coaching focus, and investment decisions only. It is not a performance review and is not to be used as the sole basis for termination, demotion, or other adverse employment actions.
