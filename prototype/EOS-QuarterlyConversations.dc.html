Discipline, meet data

Quarterly Conversations, backed by behavior.

The Quarterly Conversation is one of the best rituals in EOS. It gets even better when both sides can point to what someone actually did — initiative taken, follow-through under pressure, growth pursued — instead of trading impressions.

The short answer

Prove gives the Quarterly Conversation an objective, behavior-based reference point for capacity and growth. It makes the discussion fairer (evidence, not impressions), more forward-looking (what to develop next), and less political — all while keeping the EOS structure exactly as it is.

What behavioral data adds

Objective language

Talk about observed behavior, not personality or vibe — easier to hear and act on.

Capacity before accountability

See whether the capacity is really there before you hold someone to a bigger commitment.

A development path

Pinpoint the one trait to grow — initiative, applied grit, or learnability — and coach it deliberately.

A useful truth: some people start a Prove cycle trying to “perform” for the score. That’s fine — by the end of six weeks of real commitments, they’ve built the habits anyway. Fake it, and you tend to make it. Either way, the team gets a more committed person and you get a clearer conversation.
